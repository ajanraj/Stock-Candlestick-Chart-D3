How to Read a Candlestick Chart

A candlestick chart is a type of financial chart used to represent the price movement of an asset over time. It is commonly used in technical analysis to identify patterns and trends.

To read a candlestick chart, you need to understand the following elements:

  • Candlestick Body: The rectangular area between the opening and closing prices. It is filled (colored) if the closing price is lower than the opening price, and empty (not colored) if the closing price is higher than the opening price.
  • Candlestick Wick: The thin lines (shadows) above and below the body, representing the high and low prices during the time period.
  • Bullish Candlestick: A candlestick where the closing price is higher than the opening price. It indicates buying pressure and is often associated with a price increase.
  • Bearish Candlestick: A candlestick where the closing price is lower than the opening price. It indicates selling pressure and is often associated with a price decrease.

By analyzing the patterns and formations of candlestick charts, traders can make predictions about future price movements and make informed trading decisions.
